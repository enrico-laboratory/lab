About

It was March 2020. I was enjoying my growing career as a professional musician, choir conductor. Suddenly all my rehearsals were cancelled and all my concerts “rescheduled”. I told myself “Two or three month and I will be back to my music”. In the meanwhile, what to do with this excess of free time?

I have always been comfortable around computers. I was four years old when my father bought the first one for his company. During high school, I also tryied out some “hacker” stuff… It was a lot of fun, but it was not my path, then.

March 2020, a lot of free time and not a big pressure to immediately go back earning money. What to do? I remembered my high school IT experiments and I planned a small challenge for myself: installing Linux. I figured quite quickly that in 2020 is not such a challenge anymore. Goodbye Win10, welcome PopOS!, Fedora, Linux MX, etc…

I watched many youtube tutorials and read blog articles which gave me the basic vocabulary and an overview on the current mainstream technologies. I discovered soon enough that old fashion books would be the next step in seeking a good education, until falling in love with… official documentation, the true source of knowledge.

I was a lot of fun studying and making experiments on my home lab made of Raspberry Pis, my gaming oriented workstation and cloud instances. I was acquiring knowledge and basic experience relatively easily. I started to ask myself the question: “How much fun would it be to work in the “real world” with these amazing technologies?”.

During the summer I decided to get “serious” with it. I made a plan for the following few months. I draw a path for a basic education which would allow me to learn and practice the technologies I was passionate about and shape them with some professional coherency.

In three month I prepared and achieved the Microsoft Certified: Azure Administrator Associate (AZ-104) and the Linux Foundation Certified SysAdmin (LFCS) while studying about containers (Docker), orchestrators (Ansible) and a scripting language (Python).

I published my linkedIn profile and here I am, December 2020.
